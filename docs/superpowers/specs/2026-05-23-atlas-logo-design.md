# Atlas Logo Design

Date: 2026-05-23
Status: Proposed
Owner: Codex

## 1. Summary

Atlas needs a minimal brand mark that works both as a macOS menu bar symbol and as an app icon foundation.

The logo should not try to explain the entire product in one literal graphic. It should prioritize recognition, quiet confidence, and long-term extensibility across small and medium sizes.

The chosen direction is:

- mixed symbol rather than pure letterform
- converging information-flow concept
- quiet modern tone
- line-and-plane hybrid construction
- implicit rather than explicit core node
- slightly forward-leaning composition
- open rather than closed silhouette
- neutral graphite base with a cool teal accent

## 2. Design Goal

The logo should communicate Atlas as a calm productivity hub rather than a generic AI brand or a developer-only utility.

It must work in three practical contexts:

- menu bar scale around 16-20 px
- app icon and settings surfaces around 32-128 px
- future brand extensions such as splash, website, and documentation

The mark should feel intentional at very small sizes, with larger sizes revealing more structure instead of relying on added decoration.

## 3. Brand Meaning

The logo concept is:

`information converging toward a core`

This should be visible without drawing a literal network, orb, sparkle, or molecule.

Secondary reading:

- a subtle `A` structure
- a sense of central organization
- mild forward motion

The first read should be "stable symbol." The second read can reveal Atlas-specific meaning.

## 4. Chosen Direction

### 4.1 Form Type

The mark is a mixed symbol.

It should behave like an independent geometric sign at a glance, while preserving a quiet internal relationship to the letter `A`.

It should not read like a plain corporate monogram.

### 4.2 Structural Idea

The mark uses a line-and-plane hybrid:

- two primary planes provide stability and mass
- one lighter cut, seam, or directional stroke suggests flow
- the center is implied through negative space rather than a literal dot

The composition should lean slightly forward, but it must stop short of becoming an arrow, paper plane, or cursor icon.

### 4.3 Closure

The silhouette remains open.

The top or center should retain a small amount of breathing room so the symbol feels modern and light rather than sealed or heavy.

## 5. Visual Rules

### 5.1 Geometry

- use a simple geometric foundation
- keep the main silhouette compact
- use no more than two main planes and one lighter directional intervention
- preserve a readable center of convergence through negative space
- maintain enough asymmetry to imply motion without losing balance

### 5.2 Negative Space

The central negative space is critical. It is the implied core of the mark.

It should:

- remain visible at small sizes
- help reveal the converging structure
- contribute to the subtle `A` reading

It should not become a detached inner icon.

### 5.3 Motion

The symbol should lean slightly forward and upward.

This motion should feel like guided flow, not speed branding. Avoid exaggerated diagonals or aggressive thrust.

## 6. Color Strategy

### 6.1 Primary Use

The primary logo must succeed in monochrome.

This is required for:

- menu bar use
- template icon adaptation
- low-contrast interface contexts
- documentation and utility surfaces

### 6.2 Brand Color System

Use:

- graphite or cool dark gray as the primary structure
- a restrained cool teal as the accent

The accent color should occupy a small portion of the mark or adjacent brand system. It should not flood the entire shape.

### 6.3 Color Constraints

Avoid:

- purple-forward palettes
- rainbow gradients
- neon lighting effects
- warm orange or gold as the main identity color
- metallic rendering dependencies

## 7. Size Behavior

### 7.1 Small Sizes

At menu bar scale, the logo should read as one clean symbol with a clear silhouette and a visible central opening.

Small-size priorities:

- silhouette first
- negative space second
- subtle `A` reading third

### 7.2 Medium Sizes

At app icon and panel scale, the line-and-plane structure should become more legible.

The larger mark can reveal:

- the converging geometry
- the open silhouette
- the directional cut

But it should not rely on extra ornament to feel complete.

## 8. What the Logo Must Not Be

The logo should not become:

- a literal uppercase `A`
- a sealed triangle
- a compass arrow
- a paper plane
- a cursor pointer
- a node graph
- a molecule symbol
- a glowing AI orb
- a framed badge with a heavy border

These directions are too common, too literal, or too heavy for the desired brand tone.

## 9. Candidate Exploration Boundaries

Any visual exploration should stay inside these boundaries:

- one-family exploration, not unrelated style branches
- preserve open silhouette logic
- preserve implicit center
- preserve slight forward lean
- preserve quiet modern tone

Acceptable variation areas:

- exact angle of convergence
- proportion between planes and cut
- degree of visible `A` reading
- accent color placement

## 10. Asset Scope After Approval

Once this spec is approved, the next design artifacts should be:

1. two to three closely related black-and-white mark explorations
2. one selected primary mark
3. monochrome app icon adaptation
4. color application pass with teal accent
5. export-ready SVG and raster variants

This spec does not define typography, wordmark pairing, or a complete brand system.

## 11. Success Criteria

The logo direction succeeds if:

- it is recognizable at menu bar size
- it feels calm and modern rather than loud or generic
- it suggests convergence and central organization
- it avoids obvious AI logo cliches
- it can scale into app icon use without needing a different core symbol
